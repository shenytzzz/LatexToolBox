\chapter{First-Order Equations}

\section{Characteristic Curves}

Consider
\[
  a(x,y)u_x+b(x,y)u_y=c(x,y,u).
\]
The characteristic system
\[
  \frac{\dd x}{\dd s}=a(x,y),\qquad
  \frac{\dd y}{\dd s}=b(x,y),\qquad
  \frac{\dd u}{\dd s}=c(x,y,u)
\]
turns the PDE into a system of ordinary differential equations along curves.

\section{Transport Equation}

The constant-coefficient transport equation
\[
  u_t+v\cdot\nabla u=0
\]
has solutions that are constant along straight characteristics \(x(t)=x_0+vt\). Thus \(u(t,x)=u_0(x-vt)\).

\section{Conservation Laws}

Nonlinear conservation laws such as
\[
  u_t+f(u)_x=0
\]
may develop shocks even from smooth initial data. Weak solutions and entropy conditions are introduced to recover uniqueness.
